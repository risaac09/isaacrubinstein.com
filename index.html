Approach

Three commitments shape every engagement:

Utilization-focused. Evaluations are designed around the decisions they need to inform (Patton). Named primary users, a theory of change, and intended use drive the design from day one.

Participatory. The people closest to the program help shape the questions, interpret findings, and own the results. This produces better data and builds internal evaluation capacity at the same time.

Equity-centered. Who benefits, who is missing from the data, and whose voice shapes the narrative. These questions are built into the evaluation design, not added after the fact.

All of this sits on the CDC Framework for Program Evaluation. Agencies and funders get something they can act on: what is working, where it breaks down, what to try next.
